# Gnocchi with Peas and Sausage

A fast, one-pan meal for any night of the week. Crispy gnocchi, sweet peas, and
sausage in a light, garlicky pan sauce. Serves 4, ready in about 30 minutes.

## Ingredients

- 1 lb (450g) potato gnocchi (shelf-stable or fresh)
- 1 lb (450g) Italian sausage, casings removed
- 1 1/2 cups (200g) peas, fresh or frozen
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 small yellow onion, diced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 cup (120ml) chicken stock or pasta water
- 1/2 cup (120ml) heavy cream (optional, for a richer sauce)
- 1/2 cup (50g) grated Parmesan, plus more to serve
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- Red pepper flakes (optional)
- Fresh parsley or basil, chopped, to garnish

## Instructions

1. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the
   1 lb sausage, breaking it into bite-sized pieces, and cook until browned, 6–8
   minutes. Remove and set aside.
2. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil to the same pan. Add the 1 lb gnocchi in a single
   layer and cook undisturbed for 2–3 minutes per side until golden and
   crisp. Remove and set aside with the sausage.
3. Lower the heat to medium. Add the 1 small diced onion and cook until soft, about 3
   minutes. Add the 3 cloves minced garlic and a pinch of red pepper flakes and cook until
   fragrant, 30 seconds.
4. Pour in the 1/2 cup stock, scraping up any browned bits. Stir in the
   1/2 cup cream (if using) and bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Return the sausage and gnocchi to the pan and add the 1 1/2 cups peas. Toss and
   simmer for 2–3 minutes until the peas are tender and everything is heated
   through.
6. Off the heat, stir in the 1/2 cup Parmesan and zest of 1 lemon. Season with salt and
   pepper.
7. Serve hot, topped with extra Parmesan and fresh herbs.

## Tips

- No need to boil the gnocchi first — pan-searing straight from the package
  gives the best crispy texture.
- Swap in spicy sausage and skip the cream for a lighter, bolder version.
- A splash of pasta water loosens the sauce if it tightens up.
